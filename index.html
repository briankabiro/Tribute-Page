Tribute Page

Richard Feynman

I believe that the people we dedicate these pages to should not only be judged according to their contributions, but also what they mean to us.
  • Richard Feynman was born in Queens,NY on May 11 of 1918 to Lucille Philips and Arthur Feynman.
  • He had taught himself advanced algebra, trigonometry, analytic geometry and calculus by the time he was 15
  • He was rejected by Columbia University because of their quota of number of Jews admitted. He was accepted at MIT.
  • He attained a perfect score on the graduate school entrance exams at Princeton
  • He worked on the Manhattan Project; the team that developed the atomic bomb
  • The famous Niels Bohr usually sought him out for conversations because other phycisists were in awe of him that they couldn't argue with him
  • The FBI maintained a large file on him after one of his friends Klaus Fuchs said that he was most likely to be a spy. Ironically, Fuchs would later confess that he was a spy working for the Soviet Union
  • He claimed to be the only person to see the Trinity nuclear test without any protective glasses after he reasoned that it would be safer to watch through the windshield of a truck
  • After leaving the Manhattan project, Feynman became eligible to join the military. He faked mental illness to avoid this and the army exempted him.
  • He won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1965 together with Tomonaga and Schwinger for their fundamental work in electrodynamics
  • He recieved the Oersted Medal in 1972 and the National Medal of Science in 1979
  • His curiosity led to him learning to play the bongos and safe-cracking
